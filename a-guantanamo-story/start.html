You are a young man. You live with your parents, attend school on weekdays and work on weekends. People talk that you’re one of the brightest men your town has ever seen, that you have a future ahead of you. In the city, perhaps abroad. You’re going to do big things. It’s not just talk, either. You can feel it in your bones.

You wake up one ordinary morning, calm and resolute. Cloudless skies surround your house. You eat breakfast, hug your parents. Your dad’s hand lingers on your back for a moment, and you feel a hesitation.

Suddenly, an unnerving chill overcomes you. Your feet are rooted to the ground.

You:

go to work. stay home.
